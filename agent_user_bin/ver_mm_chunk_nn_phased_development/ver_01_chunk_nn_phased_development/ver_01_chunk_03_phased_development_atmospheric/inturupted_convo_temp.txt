Good catch on the console logs!

That exact output ([Atmosphere] WebGL fallback detected...) told us exactly what the issue was: our Orchestrator constructor was instantiating the Babylon scene synchronously, which forced it to use the default WebGL Engine instead of the advanced WebGPUEngine (which requires an asynchronous handshake with your graphics card).

I have fully rebuilt the initialization sequence. The game will now gracefully halt during start(), await a native WebGPU context from your browser, and only then build the scene and execute the dummy compile tests!

The floating-origin math test passed natively, which is also great news! (6360 - 6360 = 0) | (6460 - 6360 = 100).
